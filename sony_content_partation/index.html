Being a market leader in the headphones category, Sony India was not really concerned about only growing market share. They wanted to grow the market. The challenge was "How do we get music lovers to demand better sound quality from their audio devices?"

The core TG was music lovers. People who were listening to music on the go. Through their phones, mp3 players, laptops, car speakers etc. This is largely the segment we called the “Play List Generation”. People for whom having a particular song on their play list was more important than how it really sounded of their inferior audio devices. They were people who accepted inferior quality audio as a part of life.

Two iconic musicians – Shreya Ghoshal and Kailash Kher – came together to create a brand new song “Naina Chaar”. Their artistic process was broadcast through 9 Webisodes, where they interacted and collaborated with their fans. Through the journey, the artists showed how much effort goes into making every note perfect,
and why good quality equipment is needed to truly experience music.


Finally, their journey culminated into an online concert where the new song was unveiled.

The Project Resound microsite was the hub and the singular resource for all camapign related information and activities.
The microsite housed all the webisodes and engagements. All communication was created to direct users to the microsite.

  • Plugged N Wireless Quiz

    An enagaing quiz where fans could watch Kailash & Shreya talk about their music & life’s influences in a video on site. Fans could then participate & win in a quiz based on the videos.

  • Autograph Booth

    An interesting app which allowed fans to get closer to their favourite singers. Fans could get an autographed picture of the artist holding up their ‘biggest fan’s’ picture.

  • The Jukebox

    What better than an online concert which allows fans to pick the playlist for the concert? The Jukebox did just that as the fans voted thier favourite songs of Kailash and Shreya.

An iOS & Android based app that brought ‘Project Resound’ to mobiles. Users could simply scan a magazine ad and view an invite to the web concert. The app allowed them to pre-book their presence for the web concert. A virtual Sony headphone trial and a store locator brought the product experience right to the users’ mobiles.

6 special videos were created to share Kailash’s experience with different Sony headphones. Fans could simply scan a QR code (from their mobiles) placed on the respective products available in stores and see why Kailash love them.

13 emailers and series of web banners were created to promote the campaign and drive traffic to the microsite. These banners were run on various networks.

Fans were kept rooted to the campaign through social media handles (Facebook & twitter) of the artists and Sony India.

The campaign received extensive coverage on major news and entertainment portals and several blogs, thereby driving interest around the campaign.
